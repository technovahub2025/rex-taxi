Journey Through Tirupur: Tourist Spots
You Can't Miss

Amaravathi Dam

The Amaravathi Dam constructed across the Amaravathi River is located at Amaravathinagar, 25 kilometres south of Udumalpet on SH 17 in the Indira Gandhi Wildlife Sanctuary, Tirupur district. It is an apt spot for a picnic spot with family and friends for its sceneries, the sparkling water and enjoyable rides. Book a cab from Tirupur – Amaravati dam and enjoy lowest prices with guaranteed comfort.

Book Now

Avinashi Lingeshwarar

Known as the first among the seven shivastalams in Kongunadu, the Avinashi Lingeshwarar temple is nestled in the Chennai-Cochin NH47 highways. This places is of great historical importance and was praised by Cheras, Cholas and Pandyas. This is an important temple that is a must-visit for all Hindus and Red taxi provides an exciting outstation cab services from Tirupur to Avinashi Lingeshwarar temple

Book Now

Sivanmalai

Sivanmalai is located about 5 km away from Kangeyam in the district of Tirupur. The deity worshipped here is Lord Murugan. The temple is very famous and attracts a great number of devotees every year for the Thaipoosam festival. The “Thearotam” or the Car Festival that happens once a year lasts for three days. Visit this pilgrim spot at the affordable outstation trip package with Red taxi.

Book Now

Thirumoorthy Dam

Thirumoorthy Dam is situated 20 km away from Udumalpet on the highway from Palani to Coimbatore. The dam has been constructed adjoining the Thirumurthy Hill. This scenic dam is close to the Amanalingeswarar Temple; close by are a perennial stream and a waterfall. Watch the beautiful Anaimalai hills from the dam and enjoy a well-spent day with Red Taxi outstation trip package from Tirupur – Thirumoorthy Dam

Book Now

Vattamalai Murugan Temple

The Vattamalai Murugan Temple is another very famous religious attraction in Tirupur. It is an ancient temple dedicated to Murugan. According to legends, this temple was visited by the famous Saiva saint Arunagirinathar once. At this very spot, he praised the glory of the Lord and Kshetra in verses. Located atop a hill, Vattamalai Murugan Temple in Tamil Nadu enshrines the beautiful images of Murugan flanked by Valli and Devasena.
